# HR Analytics: Workforce & Attrition Insights

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing employee data to understand workforce composition, compensation structure, and key drivers of attrition. The goal is to identify patterns and actionable insights that can help organizations improve employee retention and optimize workforce strategies.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement
Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations, leading to increased hiring costs and productivity loss. This project aims to:
- Identify key factors influencing attrition
- Analyze workforce demographics and structure
- Understand the impact of compensation, satisfaction, and overtime on employee retention

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies
- Power BI (Data Visualization)
- DAX (Data Modeling & Measures)
- Excel / CSV Dataset (IBM HR Analytics Dataset)

---

## 📊 Dashboard Overview

### 1. Overview Dashboard
Provides a high-level summary of the workforce:
- Total Employees, Attrition Count & Rate
- Average Age, Salary, Tenure
- Workforce distribution by Department, Gender, Job Role, Education
- Salary and Experience distribution
- Attrition snapshot by Department

👉 **Key Insight:**
Identifies overall workforce structure and highlights departments with higher attrition.

---

### 2. Employee Attrition Analysis
Focuses on understanding attrition patterns:
- Attrition by Age, Education, Salary Slab
- Attrition by Job Role
- Attrition trend by Years at Company
- Job Satisfaction breakdown

👉 **Key Insight:**
Attrition is higher among certain roles and early-tenure employees.

---

### 3. Compensation & Workforce Analysis
Analyzes salary structure and workforce hierarchy:
- Average, Median, Max Salary
- Salary by Department, Role, Education
- Salary Distribution across employees
- Salary vs Experience (trend analysis)

👉 **Key Insight:**
Salary increases with experience, but varies significantly across job roles.

---

### 4. Performance & Satisfaction Analysis
Identifies drivers of employee attrition:
- Job Satisfaction & Work-Life Balance distribution
- Performance Rating distribution
- Attrition Rate by:
  - Job Satisfaction
  - Work-Life Balance
  - Overtime
  - Job Role

👉 **Key Insight:**
- Low job satisfaction → higher attrition
- Poor work-life balance → higher attrition
- Overtime significantly increases attrition risk

---

## 🔥 Key Findings
- Employees with low satisfaction have the highest attrition rate
- Overtime workers are ~3x more likely to leave
- Certain job roles (e.g., Sales, Lab roles) show higher attrition risk
- Most employees fall into mid-level performance ratings
- Salary growth is positively correlated with experience

---

## 💡 Business Impact
This analysis helps organizations:
- Identify high-risk employee groups
- Improve retention strategies
- Optimize compensation structures
- Enhance employee satisfaction and work-life balance

# 📊 HR Analytics – Workforce & Attrition Insights

## 🔍 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee data using **Power BI** to understand workforce composition, salary structure, performance, and key drivers of employee attrition.

The goal is to help organizations make **data-driven HR decisions** and reduce employee turnover.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Employee attrition leads to:

* Increased hiring and training costs
* Loss of productivity
* Reduced employee morale

👉 This project identifies **why employees leave** and provides **actionable insights to improve retention**

---

## 📂 Dataset & Tools

* 📁 Dataset: IBM HR Analytics Dataset
* 📊 Tool: Power BI
* ⚙️ Techniques:

  * Data Modeling (Star Schema)
  * DAX Measures
  * Data Visualization

---

## 📊 Dashboard Overview

### 🖼️ Overview Page

![Overview](https://github.com/AbhishekPatelAP/hr-analytics-dashboard/blob/main/Overview.png)

**Key Metrics:**

* Total Employees: **1470**
* Attrition: **237**
* Attrition Rate: **16.1%**
* Average Salary: **6.5K**
* Average Tenure: **7 Years**

**Insights:**

* Workforce is highly concentrated in **R&D (961 employees)**
* Certain departments and roles dominate workforce
* Initial signs of uneven attrition across segments

---

### 📉 Attrition Analysis

![Attrition](https://github.com/AbhishekPatelAP/hr-analytics-dashboard/blob/main/Attrition.png)

**Insights:**

* Highest attrition in **low salary group (≤5K)**
* Sales Executive (57) & Lab Technician (62) show high attrition
* Early tenure employees are more likely to leave

---

### 💰 Compensation & Workforce Analysis

![Compensation](https://github.com/AbhishekPatelAP/hr-analytics-dashboard/blob/main/Comensation and Workforce Analysis.png)

**Insights:**

* Salary increases with experience (clear trend)
* Managers & Directors earn highest salaries (~16–17K)
* Some roles are underpaid (~3K range)

---

### 😊 Performance & Satisfaction Analysis

![Performance](images/performance.png)

**Key Metrics:**

* Avg Job Satisfaction: **2.73**
* Avg Performance Rating: **3.15**
* Avg Work-Life Balance: **3**

**Insights:**

* Majority employees fall under performance rating **3 (~85%)**
* Work-life balance is mostly moderate (level 3)

---

## 🔥 Key Drivers of Attrition

* 📉 **Job Satisfaction**

  * Low (1) → **30.3% attrition**
  * High (4) → **13.9%**

* ⏱️ **Overtime**

  * Yes → **33.1%**
  * No → **10.8%**

* ⚖️ **Work-Life Balance**

  * Poor balance → highest attrition (~31%)

* 💼 **Job Role**

  * Sales Representative → **~39.8% attrition (highest)**

---

## 📌 Key Findings

* Overtime increases attrition **3x**
* Low satisfaction strongly impacts retention
* Entry-level salary group has highest attrition
* Specific roles are high-risk

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

* Reduce excessive overtime workload
* Improve employee satisfaction programs
* Focus retention strategies on high-risk roles
* Improve compensation for lower salary bands
